Fruit Vegetables

1.Cabbage
                                 


Soil & Climate: It is commonly cultivated in cool moist climate. The optimum soil temperature for seed germination is 22-26.20C and for growth is between 25.2 to 34.20C. It is grown in varied types of soils ranging from sandy loam to clay. It requires a pH ranging from 6 to 6.5 for higher production.

Cultivation:Bring the soil to a fine tilth. Pits should be taken up at a spacing of 40 cm either way in hills Ridges and furrows are formed at 45 cm apart in plains

Sowing Time: Hills- May-June, Plains- August - November

Seeds:Seed treatment with 5% Trichoderma viride. Seedling root dip with 5% Pseudomonas fluorescens before transplanting for the management of club root disease.

Sowing Rate: 200-500 g/ha

Planting Distance:Hills: 40 x 40 cm, Plains: 45 x 30 cm

Food Formulations:

Green manuring with lupin 60 days before planting
Application of decomposed farm yard manure 200 250 q/ha at the time of land preparation
Application of vermicompost @5 t/ha at the time of land preparation
Application of neem cake @ 1250 kg/ha at the time of land preparation
Application of biofertilizers, Azospirillum and Phsophobacteria @ 25 kg each/ha at the time of land preparation

Maintenance:Deep hoeing should be avoided, as the cabbage roots are surface feeders.

Water Management: First irrigation is given just after transplanting of seedlings and thereafter, irrigation may be done at 10-15 days interval according to season and soil conditions

Companion: Co, Beans, Cabbage, Cauliflower, Beets, Peas, Cucumbers, Potatoes

Plant Rotation: Plant after legumes, tomatoes and Capsicum/Simla Mirch followed by Roots, such as Onions, Potatoes, Carrots etc.

Yield:300-400 q/ha in 150 days 400-600 q/ha in 120 days

Pests-

Cut worms:

Install light trap during summer to attract adult moths

Install sprinkler irrigation system and irrigate the field in day time to expose the larvae for predation by birds

Use of pyrethrum bait prepared out of pyrethrum flowers, wheat bran and jaggery (2:1:1)

Aphids:

Spray neem oil 3%

•Spraying of 10% nettle leaf extract on 45th, 60th and 75th day after planting

Diamond backmoth:

Grow mustard as intercrop at 20:1 ratio to attract diamond back moths for oviposition.

Install pheromone traps @ 12 numbers/ha

Spray Bacillus thuringiensis @ 2 g/lit.

Release parasite Diadegma semiclausum @ 50,000/ ha, 60 days after planting

Foliar spraying of 10% garlic - chilli extract 3 times on 45th, 60th and 75th day after planting

Diseases-

Club root:

Select disease free seeds/seedlings

Seed treatment @ 10 g/kg of seeds or soil application @2.5 kg/ha or seedling dip in solution of 5g/lit with Pseudomonas fluorescens

Follow crop rotation

Crucifers should be avoided for three years

Application of dolomite @10 kg/ha should be done to increase the pH of the soil. This creates unfavourable condition for the pathogen

Leaf spot:

Foliar spray of 5% Manchurian tea filtrate 3 times at one month interval from one month after sowing/ planting

Foliar spray of 3% Dasagavya at 10 days interval from 1st month after planting Seeds should be soaked in hot water (50oC) for 30. minutes, dried and then sown.



Leaf Blight



Spraying Agni Hotra ash (200 g Agni Hotra ash soaked in 1 liter cow urine for 15 days and diluted in 10 liters of water before spraying) 3 times at one month interval from one month after sowing/planting.

Black rot:

Dip the seeds in 100 ppm Streptocycline for 30 minutes

Two sprays with Streptomycin 100 ppm after planting and at head formation

Soil boe diseases:

Application of Trichoderma viride @ 5 kg/ha at the time of land preparation

Application of Pseudomonas fluorescens @ 5 kg/ha at the time of land preparation

2.Cauliflower
                                 


Soil & Climate: Cultivation of cauliflower is wide spread in many countries of the world. The optimum temperature for growth of young plants is around 230 C, but in later stages 17-200 C are most favorable. A fairly deep loamy soil is most desirable. It is sensitive to high acidity. The optimum soil pH for maximum production is 5.5-.6.

Cultivation:Polyhouse may be utilized to provide favourable climate for germination of seeds and protection of seedlings from adverse weather condition. Four to six weeks old seedlings are transplanted in the field.

Sowing Time: The sowing of seeds is done in May-June for early season, July-August for midseason and September October for late season varieties

Seeds: The seeds of Cauliflower are sown in raised nursery-beds of sterilized soil. For early season crop the seeds should be treated with some mercurial fungicide to save the young seedlings from damping off.

Sowing Rate: The seed rate is about 1 kg/ha for early varieties and 200 to 500 g/ha for late varieties.

Planting Distance: Seedlings of early varieties are planted to 60 cm row to row and 30-45 cm plant to plant distance. The late varieties are planted at spacing of 60-75 cm between rows and 45-60 cm between plants.

Food Formulations:

150-200 q/ha of FYM along with N, P, K fertilizers @ 60 kg N, 80 kg P205, and 40 kg K20 per hectare should be applied before transplanting of cauliflower seedlings.

Maintenance: Frequent shallow hoeing is practiced for the control of weeds Black plastic mulch is another effective method to control weeds, because it prevents light and restricts favorable conditions for the growth and germination of weeds.

Water Management: First irrigation is given just after transplanting First irrigation is given just after transplanting. For early and mid season crop, usually. lesser number of irrigation is needed because of rains

Companion:Basil, Bean, Dill, Garlic, Lettuce, Marigold, Mint, Onion.

Plant Rotation: Plant after tomato/brinjal/potato and before pea/bean.

Yield: Early maturing cultivars-80-120 q/ha, Main season cauliflower -150-200 q/ha.

Cauliflower Pests and Problems: The control measures for insects, pests and diseases depend upon type and intensity of the problems. The control measures for the main pests and diseases are described below.

Cabbage Maggot:It attacks on the young rootlets and main root and causes wilting of plants. Application of suspension of Calomelis has been recommended as a control measure.

Damping off: It is a common disease in the nursery-beds of the early cauliflower. It is controlled by soil solarization using transparent plastic film or drenching the nursery beds with fungicide 3 to 4 weeks before sowing of seeds.

Downy Mildew: It is characterized by the appearance of purplish-brown spots on the lower side of the leaves. The causal organism is an obligate parasite, which is present in the soil. Sanitation and crop rotation help in reducing the infection of Downy mildew.

3.Brinjal

                                 


Soil & Climate: Aubergine or Eggplant needs steady, warm growing conditions. Well-drained and fertile soil is preferred for the crop with pH between5.5-6.6. Eggplant performs best in daytime temperatures of 25-33 C and night time temperatures no lower than 20°C

Sowing time: March-April Plantation time: April-May.

Seed Rate: approx 300 to 500 grams/ Ha,

Sowing Distance:90 x 75 cm

Food formulations:
25 tonnes of farm yard manure loosen the planting bed and mix in a 2-inch layer of rich compost made of cow-dunk fertilizer and then water well. in cool climates, cover the prepared bed with blac plastic film to help warm the soil. Allow 24 inches between plants

Maintenance :Weeding after 15 to 20 days at regular intervals.

Water Management: Irrigation is required at every third or 4th day during summer while in winter it should be at 10-15 days interval.

Companion plant: Beans, Carrot, Fenugreek, Cabbage.

Plant Rotation: After - Beet & Carrot and Before. Cauliflower & Broccoli

Yield: 30-40 ton per Ha.

Integrated Pest and Disease Management:

Nursery- two sprays of 4% neem liquid mix pesticides to the plant at an interval of 10 days.

After plantation-

1. Mix cow-dunk Compost or neem cake with the soil. 10 days after planting.

2. 10 pieces per acre of Pheromone (smell) traps.

3. Get rid of the diseased tops.

4. 30 days after planting, spray about 4% neem of liquid mix pesticide every 10 days of interval.
